How to Serve a Late Rent Notice in Colorado

Colorado law (Colorado Revised Statutes Section 13-40-104) governs how notices must be delivered. Accepted methods include:

  • 1Personal service — Hand the notice directly to the tenant. This is the most reliable method and starts the clock immediately.
  • 2Certified mail — Send via USPS certified mail with return receipt. Keep the receipt as proof of delivery. Note that the notice period may not begin until the tenant receives it.
  • 3Posting and mailing — Post the notice in a conspicuous place on the property AND mail a copy the same day. Both steps are required for this method to be valid.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did Colorado's notice period change from 3 to 10 days?

Colorado's SB 23-184, signed in 2023, extended the non-payment notice period from 3 to 10 days as part of broader housing stability legislation. The law also added new requirements for the content and delivery of notices.

Are there additional requirements for the notice content in Colorado?

Yes — Colorado now requires notices to include information about tenant rights and available legal resources. Using an outdated template that doesn't include this information can invalidate your notice.

Can Denver have different rules than the rest of Colorado?

Denver and other municipalities can adopt additional tenant protections beyond state minimums. Denver has enacted several local ordinances — always check current Denver Revised Municipal Code if your property is within city limits.

What happens after the 10-day period in Colorado?

If the tenant hasn't paid or vacated, you file a Forcible Entry and Detainer (FED) action in County Court. Colorado courts have been backlogged since the pandemic — expect 2-4 weeks before a hearing date.
